What is IVF?

Turning Hope Into Reality

In Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) is a series of medical procedures used to help with fertility or prevent genetic problems and assist with the conception of a child. During IVF, mature eggs are retrieved from ovaries and fertilised by sperm in a lab.

The fertilised egg — or embryo — is then implanted in the uterus. One full cycle of IVF takes about 2 to 3 weeks, and sometimes multiple cycles are needed.

The IVF Process — Step by Step

1
Initial Consultation & Testing

Comprehensive hormonal blood tests, semen analysis and pelvic ultrasound to assess your fertility baseline.

2
Ovarian Stimulation

Daily hormone injections (8–12 days) stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple mature eggs, monitored by serial ultrasounds.

3
Egg Retrieval (OPU)

A minor procedure under light sedation where eggs are aspirated from follicles using an ultrasound-guided needle.

4
Fertilisation in the Lab

Eggs and sperm are combined in our state-of-the-art embryology lab. Fertilisation is confirmed after 18 hours.

5
Embryo Culture & Selection

Embryos are cultured for 3–5 days. Our embryologists use time-lapse imaging and AI tools to select the best embryo.

6
Embryo Transfer

One or two embryos are placed into the uterus via a fine catheter — a painless, 10-minute procedure with no anaesthesia.

7
Pregnancy Test & Follow-up

A blood test 14 days after transfer confirms pregnancy. Your journey with our team continues through early pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is IVF suitable for? +

IVF is recommended for couples with blocked or damaged fallopian tubes, severe male infertility, unexplained infertility, endometriosis, PCOS, or those who have had multiple failed IUI cycles. Your doctor will assess your specific situation to determine if IVF is the right path.

How long does one IVF cycle take? +

A fresh IVF cycle typically takes 4–6 weeks from the first day of your period to the pregnancy test. This includes stimulation (8–12 days), egg retrieval, fertilisation, embryo culture (3–5 days), a brief freeze-all or transfer, and the 2-week wait.

What is the cost of IVF at Bloom? +

IVF costs vary based on age, diagnosis, and treatment protocol. At Bloom IVF, packages start from ₹1,20,000 inclusive of monitoring scans and lab fees. Our counsellors provide a transparent, itemised quote with no hidden charges during your consultation.

Is IVF painful? +

Most patients experience mild discomfort from the daily injections and some bloating during stimulation. The egg retrieval is done under sedation so you will not feel pain. The embryo transfer is similar to a smear test — brief and generally pain-free.

What happens to unused embryos? +

Good-quality surplus embryos are vitrified (flash-frozen) and stored for future frozen embryo transfer (FET) cycles. This significantly improves cumulative success rates and reduces the physical and financial burden of additional stimulation cycles.
